Offsides Explained

Welcome to the more in-depth portion of our learning platform. Start discovering the specific rules of offsides in soccer!

Basic of offsides rules

In soccer, a player is OFFSIDE if they are nearer to the opponent's goal than both the ball and their second-to-last opponent when the ball is played to them, unless they are in their own half of the field.

Offsides in action

Key moment to look at when determining offsides: interfering with the play at the moment when the ball is passed

Remember: only the team with the ball can be offside, and offside can only happen in the attacking part of the field

The body can complicate offside rules

A player is NOT offside even if their hands/arms are in offside position as long as their head, body, and feet are in onside position.
